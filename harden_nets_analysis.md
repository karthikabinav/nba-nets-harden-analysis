# Brooklyn Nets - James Harden Trade Impact Analysis

## Question
Did the Nets score more points in their first regular season game with James Harden than in their first regular season game of the previous season? What was the point difference between those two games?

## Background
James Harden was traded to the Brooklyn Nets on **January 13, 2021** from the Houston Rockets in a blockbuster four-team trade. This analysis compares the Nets' offensive performance in Harden's debut game versus their season opener from the previous year.

## Key Dates & Games

### James Harden's First Game with the Brooklyn Nets
- **Date:** January 16, 2021
- **Opponent:** Orlando Magic
- **Location:** Barclays Center (Home)
- **Final Score:** Brooklyn Nets 122 - Orlando Magic 115
- **Nets Points:** 122
- **Context:** This was just 3 days after the trade was completed. Harden recorded a triple-double in his debut: 32 points, 12 rebounds, and 14 assists.

### Brooklyn Nets First Game of 2019-20 Season (Previous Season)
- **Date:** October 23, 2019
- **Opponent:** Minnesota Timberwolves
- **Location:** Barclays Center (Home)
- **Final Score:** Minnesota Timberwolves 127 - Brooklyn Nets 126 (OT)
- **Nets Points:** 126
- **Context:** This was the season opener for the 2019-20 NBA season. Kyrie Irving had just joined the Nets in free agency.

## Comparison Results

| Game | Date | Opponent | Nets Points |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| First game WITH Harden (2020-21) | Jan 16, 2021 | Orlando Magic | 122 |
| First game of PREVIOUS season (2019-20) | Oct 23, 2019 | Minnesota Timberwolves | 126 |

### Answer
**NO**, the Nets did NOT score more points in their first game with James Harden.

### Point Difference
The Nets scored **4 fewer points** in Harden's debut game compared to their previous season opener.

**Calculation:** 122 - 126 = -4 points

## Analysis

Despite the narrative that Harden would immediately boost the Nets' offense, the team actually scored fewer points in his debut game (122) than in their previous season opener (126). However, context is important:

1. **Different Circumstances:** The 2019-20 opener went to overtime, which provided extra playing time to score points
2. **New Team Chemistry:** Harden was playing his first game with new teammates after just 3 days
3. **Still a Strong Performance:** 122 points is still a very strong offensive output
4. **Long-term Impact:** While the first game didn't show an immediate scoring increase, Harden's presence eventually helped form one of the most potent offenses in NBA history alongside Kevin Durant and Kyrie Irving

While the Brooklyn Nets did not score more points in James Harden's first game with the team compared to their previous season opener (scoring 4 fewer points), this single-game comparison doesn't fully capture Harden's long-term offensive impact on the franchise.
